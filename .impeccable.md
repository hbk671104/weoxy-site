## Design Context

### Users

DME (Durable Medical Equipment) dealers and distributors evaluating portable oxygen concentrators for their product catalog. These are business buyers who compare specs, margins, and support infrastructure across manufacturers. They browse the site during work hours at a desk, often comparing WeOxy side-by-side with competitors like Inogen. They care about clinical data, logistics (shipping, warranty, replacement speed), and ease of ordering. Prices are gated behind login — the public site's job is to build credibility and drive dealer inquiries.

### Brand Personality

**Reliable · Professional · Intuitive**

WeOxy should feel like the manufacturer that has its act together — clear specs, no BS, fast support, devices that just work. The emotional register is confidence without arrogance. A dealer landing on this site should think: "These people know what they're doing and they'll be easy to work with."

### Aesthetic Direction

**Clinical precision** — clean, confident, data-driven. The interface should feel like a well-organized spec sheet elevated to a modern web experience. Think: the clarity of a medical instrument display, the trust of a diagnostic report, the efficiency of a well-designed procurement portal.

- **Theme**: Light. This is a daytime work tool for business buyers. Dark hero sections are acceptable for visual contrast, but the dominant experience should be light.
- **Color palette**: Navy and teal as the foundation, open to refinement. Tint neutrals toward the brand hue for cohesion. Avoid the generic "medical teal + white" look — the palette should feel considered and distinctive within the medical device space.
- **Typography**: Professional, highly legible, with clear hierarchy. Body text must scan easily in spec-heavy sections. Display type should convey authority without being flashy.
- **References**: Inogen Provider portal (provider.inogen.com) — clean, clinical, provider-focused structure. Rhythm HC (rhythmhc.com) — modern medical device brand feel.
- **Anti-references**: Generic AI-generated medical sites with cyan gradients. Consumer-facing oxygen concentrator sites that lean too emotional/lifestyle. Anything that looks like a Tailwind template.

### Design Principles

1. **Specs over sizzle** — Lead with concrete data. Every section should communicate something measurable. Dealers evaluate products on numbers, not vibes.
2. **Scannable trust** — Certifications, warranty terms, and logistics details should be instantly findable, not buried. Build credibility through structure, not decoration.
3. **Quiet confidence** — The design should be restrained and precise. No gimmicks, no unnecessary animation, no decorative elements that don't serve comprehension. Let the product's superiority speak through clean presentation.
4. **Professional efficiency** — Respect the buyer's time. Clear navigation, logical information hierarchy, no redundant sections. The site should feel like it was designed by someone who understands B2B procurement.
5. **Distinctive within medical** — Avoid the trap of "medical = boring." The site should feel elevated and modern within the medical device category without crossing into consumer lifestyle territory.
